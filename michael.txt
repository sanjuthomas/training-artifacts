Basic Metrics to learn:

ROA - Return on Assets
ROE - Return on Equity
ROIC - Return on invested capital
ROCE - Return on capital employed

If the above four are greater than 15% is a great company. That doesn't mean the balance sheet isn't leveraged. So, next, look at the debt-to-equity ratio.

DE - Debt equity 
<1 is great and < 1.5 is good 

Next - look at what you pay to own it?

PE - Price to earnings
Depends on the sector: <25 in tech is great. 
<10 for utility is great.

Why should you look at the PE? You can buy a great company for a bad price. You want to avoid that; that is why Buffett says, when others are fearful, you must be greedy.

How would you assess the intrinsic value? You need to know how much money they are making now and the projected cash flow.

FCF - Free Cash Flow (Historical or current performance.)
DCF - Discounted Cash Flow (Future potential.)
